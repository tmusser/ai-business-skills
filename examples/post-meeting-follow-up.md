# Post-Meeting Follow-Up Example

## Situation

You finished a launch meeting and need to turn the transcript into a sendable follow-up.

## Messy Input

```text
Transcript notes:
- Launch is still on track for Friday.
- Engineering said the metrics dashboard is ready, but the backfill is not.
- PM said "we need someone to own the comms draft."
- Customer success asked whether the late change will affect the pilot group.
- Nobody confirmed who is sending the final update.
```

## What The Skill Notices

- The meeting has decisions and open loops, not just notes.
- There is an owner gap on the comms draft.
- The final update sender is still unclear.
- The pilot-group impact question needs a response in the follow-up.

## Clean Output

- Decisions made: Friday launch is still the plan.
- Open decisions: who sends the final update and whether the pilot group needs a change notice.
- Action items: assign an owner for the comms draft and confirm the final sender.
- Blockers: the backfill is not ready yet.
- Follow-up draft: "Thanks everyone. Friday is still the launch target. We have the dashboard ready, but the backfill is still in progress. We still need an owner for the comms draft and a decision on whether the pilot group needs a change note. Please reply with who is taking the final update."
- Source gaps: the transcript does not say who owns the final send.

## Why This Is Better Than A Generic AI Reply

- It turns a meeting into decisions and actions instead of a prose recap.
- It keeps the owner gap visible.
- It gives you a draft that is ready to send with minimal editing.

## Source Gaps

- The final sender is not named.
- The pilot-group answer is implied but not resolved.
